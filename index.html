Autonomous Gel Blaster Turret

For this project, the goal was to develop an autonomous turret that when powered, will scan the area for a target and fine adjust the motor control to point the toy gun barrel at a pre-determined target signature. Despite the lack of certain sensor feedback, the turret itself was able to effectively locate a target located in the blind-spot ranges of the barrel and adjust accordingly before firing. For my role, I was heavily involved in the software and coding of the behavior and control loops such as the FSM that is used. Additionally, I offered some insight for the mechanical structure using knowledge from when I was studying mechanical engineering in community college.

Autonomous Vehicle Platform

This project had students create an autonomous vehicular platform to perform in four events as follows: Tug of War, Race, Trade Depot, and Hide n' Seek. To accomplish these tasks, our robot utilized a closed feedback loop for Tug of War, line following for the Race, and color/signature recognition for both Trade Depot and Hide n' Seek. This project tasked me with working on software for sensor fusion and feedback data sent to the STM32 Nucleo board to adjust motor control. Additionally, I had done some work in the mechanical design to ensure the design fit everything within the size constraint we were given as well as I was involved in the electrical design.

FPGA Chess/Checkers Implementation

This project's overall goal was to prove the student's knowledge of FPGAs and Verilog by creating something from the ground up. In this case, Chess was a heavy influence and was coded in Verilog with the VGA modules being coded in HDL. The end goal of the project was to create a fully-functional game of chess between two players or against an AI opponent with the logic for all rules to be implemented. The group was able to accomplish two-player play however was not able to include an AI opponent due to time constraints. Due to time constraints as well, the project pivoted towards a Checkers implementation instead. The project is scalable to include both chess and AI capabilities however. For my portion, I was heavily responsible for creating the all of the game/board logic and tying up all the modules together into the top module to be functioning.
